# Course Structure

## Overall logic
The course is built so that the student gradually follows this path:
1. Understand how signals and SDR chains work.
2. Learn to receive and analyze real signals.
3. Use MATLAB / Simulink for modeling.
4. Prepare algorithms for fixed-point and HDL implementation.
5. Understand the FPGA/SoC and RF hardware route.
6. Add circuit design and KiCad.
7. Build an integrated project.

## Blocks
### Block 1
Introduction to SDR, software, training setup, KiCad, and first reception of a test signal.

### Block 2
Signals, spectrum, sampling, complex representation, sample rate, aliasing.

### Block 3
Basic DSP operations: FFT, windows, filtering, frequency estimation, digital frequency shifting.

### Block 4
Simulink models, fixed-point, scaling, preparation for HDL.

### Block 5
HDL flow, FPGA implementation, Vivado/Vitis, SoC integration, verification.

### Block 6
RF frontend, levels, frequency, bandwidth, matching, AD9363.

### Block 7
Transmit and receive chains, DUC/DDC, generators, processing pipeline.

### Block 8
Modulation and synchronization: BPSK/QPSK/FSK, carrier and timing recovery, basic demodulation.

### Block 9
Recording and analysis tools: HDSDR, MATLAB, Simulink, Python, C++, GNU Radio.

### Block 10
KiCad, breadboard work, generators, analog and digital support circuits for experiments.

### Block 11
Integrated educational SDR project with a full route from model to board and analysis.

### Block 12
Final projects and possible extensions of the course.

## Recommended cadence
- 1 theory part
- 1 demonstration
- 1 lab
- 1 report / discussion of results
